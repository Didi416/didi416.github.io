## Solar-Powered Coral Terrarium: Innovating for Research and Culture
### The Challenge

In 2025, my team for this subject, 'Green Energy', took on an exciting challenge from Engineers Without Borders: to design a solar-powered coral terrarium. This wasn't just any aquarium project - it was a chance to make a real difference for coral research, Indigenous culture, and education in Far North Queensland.


### Our Innovative Solution
![image-left](/assets/images/I2EP_D4.jpg){: .align-left}We developed a portable, sustainable terrarium that:
- Runs entirely on solar power
- Provides optimal conditions for short-term coral storage
- Offers an enhanced viewing experience for researchers and the public

### Key Features:

- **Cutting-edge solar technology:** Our design would be able to operate for 3 days without sunlight using intelligent power management.
- **Perfect coral habitat:** We carefully balanced water chemistry, lighting, and temperature control.
- **Cultural sensitivity:** The design respects and supports Indigenous connections to sea country.
- **Research-ready:** Facilitates easy observation and study of coral specimens.

### Our Process

1. **Deep research:** We dove into environmental factors, Indigenous culture, and coral ecology.
2. **Creative ideation:** We developed multiple design concepts, from external solar panels to information board integration.
3. **Rigorous evaluation:** Using design matrices, we objectively assessed each option.
4. **Synthesis and refinement:** We combined the best elements into our final, optimized design.

### Achievements

- **Cost-effective:** Initial build cost of ~$1200, with weekly upkeep under $10 (estimated).
- **Sustainable:** Triple bottom line approach considering environmental, social, and economic factors.
- **Community-focused:** Implementation plan includes local engagement and training.

### Impact

Our solar-powered coral terrarium isn't just a technical achievement - it's a bridge between science, culture, and education. It provides researchers with a vital tool, helps preserve Indigenous knowledge, and offers a window into the underwater world for students and the public.

### The Team's Growth

This project pushed us to:
- Think creatively about complex, real-world problems
- Develop our collaboration and project management skills
- Gain a deeper appreciation for interdisciplinary approaches
